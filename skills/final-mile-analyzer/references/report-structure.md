# Report Structure — sections, charts, copy patterns

The report renders top-to-bottom in this exact order. Each section maps to a key in the report JSON payload (see `schemas/report.schema.json`). To reorder, change order here AND in the schema's `section_order` array.

All chart colour conventions:
- **Current series**: Aggregate Teal `#81a0aa`
- **Projected / new series**: Aggregate Tiger `#f2663b`
- **Savings / positive deltas**: Aggregate Tiger
- **Negative deltas / risk**: warm grey `#a08570` (don't use red — it reads aggressive in client decks)
- **Reference lines / structure**: Aggregate Blue `#2c3e52`

---

## Section 1 — Header

**Schema key**: `header`

Content:
- Tenant name (large)
- Period analysed (e.g. "Mar 2026" or "Q1 2026 average")
- Total monthly shipments
- Total monthly spend (current)
- Confidence score (0-100, with a 1-line interpretation)

No chart. Pure summary cards.

---

## Section 2 — Headline Savings

**Schema key**: `headline_savings`

The single most important number in the report. Render as a centred hero block.

Content:
- Annual savings (AUD, big number, Aggregate Tiger colour)
- Monthly savings (AUD, smaller)
- Savings % (one decimal place)
- Current annual spend → projected annual spend (delta arrow)
- One-sentence framing — generated by Claude in the report prompt — that translates the number into a decision e.g. "Switching to the proposed Australia Post card unlocks $142,000 / yr without changing carrier mix."

No chart in this section. The next section gives the visual.

---

## Section 3 — Current vs Projected Spend (chart)

**Schema key**: `spend_comparison`

**Chart type**: grouped bar chart, two bars per group:
- X axis: carrier + service combinations (e.g. "AusPost Standard", "StarTrack Premium")
- Y axis: monthly spend AUD
- Series 1 ("Current"): Aggregate Teal
- Series 2 ("Projected"): Aggregate Tiger
- Sort groups by current spend desc

Below the chart, render a table with columns:
- Carrier
- Service
- Monthly shipments
- Current CPO
- Projected CPO
- CPO delta (AUD + %)
- Monthly savings
- Annual savings

Sort table same as chart.

---

## Section 4 — State / Region Breakdown

**Schema key**: `region_breakdown`

**Chart type**: horizontal stacked bar chart per region — segments showing current vs projected spend per region. Optional: a small map indicator if states are detected.

Below, a table with columns:
- Region (state if detected, otherwise zone group)
- Share of shipments (%)
- Monthly shipments
- Current spend
- Projected spend
- Savings (AUD + %)

If `Unmapped` rows are >10% of total shipments, render a warning callout above the section linking to the sanity-checks section: "X% of shipments couldn't be assigned to a region — see warnings."

---

## Section 5 — Cubic Meter Analysis

**Schema key**: `cube_analysis`

Two visual blocks side-by-side on desktop, stacked on mobile:

### 5a. Cube totals
- Total monthly cubic meters shipped
- Total annual cubic meters
- Cost per m³ (current)
- Cost per m³ (projected)
- Cost per m³ delta

If `avg_cube_m3` was estimated (not measured), include a small note: "Cube estimated from weight at {DEFAULT_DENSITY_KG_PER_M3} kg/m³ — capture actual cube data for precision."

### 5b. DIM-billed risk lanes
A short table of (carrier, service, zone) lanes flagged as DIM-billed risk per [`metrics.md`](./metrics.md) §5. Columns:
- Lane
- Avg weight (kg)
- Estimated DIM weight (kg)
- Risk note (one line)

If no DIM risk detected, suppress 5b and show a single line in 5a: "No DIM-billed risk detected at default density."

---

## Section 6 — Top 5 Savings Opportunities

**Schema key**: `top_opportunities`

**Chart type**: horizontal bar chart, one bar per opportunity, length = annual savings AUD, colour = Aggregate Tiger.

Below the chart, a list view (not a table) showing for each opportunity:
- Rank (1-5)
- Lane name (e.g. "AusPost Standard — Metro NSW")
- Annual savings (AUD, bold)
- Per-parcel delta (e.g. "−$1.40 per parcel")
- Volume affected (parcels/month)
- One-line consultant note generated by Claude — what's driving the gap (e.g. "Old card priced flat; new card has weight tiers under 1kg.")

---

## Section 7 — Recommendations

**Schema key**: `recommendations`

A bulleted list of action items, generated by Claude. Each recommendation has:
- A short title (e.g. "Migrate Metro NSW Standard to new card")
- A one-paragraph rationale
- Estimated impact (AUD/yr) where applicable

Bias toward 3-7 recommendations. More than that and the consultant's eyes glaze over.

---

## Section 8 — Sanity Check Warnings

**Schema key**: `warnings`

Render as a callout block at the bottom (or near the relevant section). Each warning has:
- Severity (`info` | `warn` | `block`)
- Title
- Body (one or two sentences)
- Linked section if relevant

Sort by severity (block → warn → info). If any `block` warnings exist, render them prominently at the top of the report too — these are things the consultant must address before sharing externally.

See [`sanity-checks.md`](./sanity-checks.md) for the full list of triggers.

---

## Section 9 — Methodology footer

**Schema key**: `methodology`

A collapsed "How we calculated this" block. Pulls a summarised version of [`metrics.md`](./metrics.md) so the client can audit the numbers. Includes:
- Default density used for cube
- How surcharges are handled (currently: not modelled)
- How unmatched rows are handled (held flat)
- Confidence score formula in plain English

Always include this — it's a trust signal.

---

## Copy patterns

- Numbers are decisions. Always lead with the dollar number, then the lane, then the why.
- Avoid hedge words ("might", "could potentially") in headline copy. The methodology footer already covers caveats.
- Annual savings always trumps monthly in headlines. Monthly is detail.
- "Projected" not "predicted" — cleaner, more deterministic-sounding.
- Never call the new card "better" or "worse" — call it "lower-cost" or "higher-cost" for the volume mix.
